Matilda Sutherland is a songwriter, producer and DJ based in Narrm. Adopting the MTLDA moniker in two-thousand and eighteen, her relationship with DJing has since become heavily centered around the energy put forth by the crowd. Enjoying the push and pull of intimacy between dancer and performer, the way a DJ can hold, lose, and then regain the audience’s captivation at any given time. Sutherland's late night club sets mostly consist of off-kilter psychedelia, hard-hitting rhythms, and a dark conjuring of movement.

Described by Triple j as a rising talent in Melbourne's underground scene, Sutherland has contributed mixes for Melbourne labels Steeplejack and Butter Sessions, maintaining a steady output of mixes and radio appearances. Sutherland has graced the stages of Hugs&Kisses, Lounge and Sub Club countless times, she has supported international artists the likes of Function, SPFDJ and Wata Igarashi - both solo and as part of back to back duo with Nat Salih. This year marked Matilda's first solo release via the MTLDA moniker, with a rhythmic minimal techno track exploring acid inspired synthesis, Gaba.



Girl On Road is Matilda latest project, which she plans to morph into a record label via her own forthcoming release. What began as a digital transmission via Skylab Radio has become an opportunity to support artists across the globe who are pushing boundaries in their music, through heavily anarchic undertones and overt politcal statements.

In twenty twenty-one Matilda plans to extend herself beyond electronic music, with a solo EP to be released under her birth name. With intentions to build a rich career as a performing artist and composer on top of her DJing moniker, the EP will explore Matilda's non-electronic influences. Drawing upon experience of growing up surrounded by folk and rock music, the release is a display of Matilda's deep and varied love for music.
